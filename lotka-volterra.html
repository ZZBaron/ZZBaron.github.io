Project Overview

This project implements and visualizes the Lotka-Volterra predator-prey model and its enhanced variants. It shows the cyclical dynamics between predator and prey populations over time.

Key Features

The simulation includes:

  • Basic Lotka-Volterra predator-prey model implementation
  • A more biologically realistic model with additional ecological factors
  • Population dynamics visualization over time
  • Analysis of functional response curves with predator interference
  • Population ratio dynamics
  • Animated phase space visualization

Lotka-Volterra Model

The Lotka-Volterra model consists of a pair of first-order, non-linear differential equations that describe the dynamics of biological systems with predator-prey interactions:

\[ \frac{dx}{dt} = \alpha x - \beta xy \]

\[ \frac{dy}{dt} = \delta xy - \gamma y \]

where:

  • \(x\) is the Prey population
  • \(y\) is the Predator population
  • \(\alpha\) is the Prey growth rate
  • \(\beta\) is the Predation rate
  • \(\gamma\) is the Predator death rate
  • \(\delta\) is the Predator growth rate due to consuming prey

A More Realistic Model

We can modify the Lotka-Volterra model to incorporate several ecological factors that make the simulation more biologically realistic:

Seasonal Variations

We can vary parameters seasonally according to a :

\[ S = 1 + A \cdot \sin(2\pi t/12) \]

where \(S\) is the seasonal factor and \(A\) is the seasonal amplitude.

Prey Logistic Growth

Instead of unlimited exponential growth, prey follows logistic growth constrained by carrying capacity \(K\):

\[ \frac{dx}{dt} = rx(1-\frac{x}{K}) - S y f(x,y) \]

where \(r\) is the intrinsic growth rate and \(f(x,y)\) is the functional response.

Holling Type II Functional Response

Models predator satiation, where predation rate doesn't increase linearly with prey density:

\[ f(x,y) = \frac{ax}{1 + ahx + qy} \]

where:

  • \(a\): Attack rate
  • \(h\): Handling time per prey
  • \(q\): Predator interference coefficient

Predator Dynamics

With conversion efficiency and mortality:

\[ \frac{dy}{dt} = c S y f(x,y) - my \]

where \(c\) is the conversion efficiency and \(m\) is the mortality rate.

Time Lag

The model incorporates a time lag in predator response, reflecting realistic delays in population dynamics.

System Properties

The Lotka-Volterra system exhibits several important properties:

  • Oscillatory behavior: Predator and prey populations cycle continuously in the basic model
  • Phase space orbits: Closed loops in the phase space representing periodic solutions
  • Conservation principle: The basic model conserves a quantity related to the total energy of the system
  • Stability: The enhanced model can exhibit stable equilibria under certain parameter regimes

Applications

Lotka-Volterra models and their extensions have widespread applications in:

  • Ecological population dynamics
  • Fisheries management and sustainable harvesting
  • Wildlife conservation and endangered species protection
  • Disease spread modeling with hosts and pathogens
  • Biological control of pest species
  • Economic systems with competing products
